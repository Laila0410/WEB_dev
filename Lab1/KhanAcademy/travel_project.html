Stand in awe before the legendary Mount Everest, the highest mountain on Earth and the ultimate symbol of human ambition.
Rising 8,848 meters above sea level, Everest towers over the Himalayas on the border between Nepal and Tibet. Its snow-covered peaks and dramatic ridges define nature at its most powerful and majestic.
The mountain is known as Sagarmatha in Nepal and Chomolungma in Tibet, both names honoring it as a sacred mother of the world. The extreme altitude, freezing temperatures, and thin air make Everest one of the most challenging places on the planet.
From breathtaking viewpoints in the Khumbu region to the iconic Everest Base Camp, visitors are rewarded with unforgettable views of glaciers, icefalls, and towering Himalayan peaks. Every angle offers a reminder of the raw beauty and scale of the world’s highest mountain.
